## Key Mental Models

1. **The Value Equation**: Every offer's irresistibility can be measured. (Dream × Success) / (Time × Effort). If the score is low, you know exactly which component to fix.

2. **The Problem-Solution Chain**: Every solution reveals a new problem. Your Money Model is a chain of offers, each solving the problem the previous offer revealed. Map the chain, then make an offer at every link.

3. **The Pretzel Model (Lead Magnets)**: Free/cheap thing solves a narrow problem and creates a new desire → paid thing satisfies that desire. Salty pretzels → thirst → drinks.

4. **The Core Four 2×2 Matrix**: Warm/Cold × 1-to-1/1-to-many = 4 ways to get leads. Start warm, scale cold. Start 1-to-1, scale 1-to-many.

5. **The Leverage Ladder**: You do it (Core Four) → Others do it (Lead Getters) → Machines do it (Automation). Each step multiplies your reach without multiplying your time.

6. **Perception > Reality**: The Value Equation components are all about *perceived* value. You don't need to change your product — change how people perceive the four components.

## Critical Principles

1. **Make offers so good people feel stupid saying no** — this is the bar for every offer, including free ones.
2. **Downselling ≠ Discounting** — never drop your price just to close a sale. Change WHAT they get or HOW they pay.
3. **Give away the secrets, sell the implementation** — 99% won't buy, but they'll create (or destroy) your reputation based on your free stuff.
4. **Prove the model yourself before hiring others** — if you can't generate leads profitably, no lead getter can either.
5. **Any reason > no reason** — Harvard research shows people comply more when given ANY reason, even a weak one. Always include a "because."
6. **More money faster = more growth** — the faster customers pay you, the faster you can reinvest and grow profitably.
7. **The fastest path to a higher Offer Score**: bold guarantee (↑Success), quick win (↓Time), done-for-you (↓Effort).

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- Single-offer business (only selling one thing)
- Discounting in the moment out of fear
- Making lead magnets that are "sucky fluff"
- Hiring agencies before proving the channel yourself
- Hiding your price instead of improving your offer
- Offering upsells that don't solve the revealed next problem
- Not tracking cost-per-lead and conversion rate
- Creating content without a CTA
