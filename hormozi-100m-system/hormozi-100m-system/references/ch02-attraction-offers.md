# Chapter 2: Attraction Offers — Turn Eyeballs Into Money

## Core Idea
Attraction Offers generate leads AND convert them into customers by offering something free or at a steep discount. They turn advertising into money, solving the #1 problem: getting customers without going broke.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Win Your Money Back**: Pay now with a chance to get your money back later if you meet criteria (results, actions, or both). Generated $1B+ in sales industry-wide.
- **Giveaways**: Advertise a grand prize → collect contact info → everyone else gets a promotional discount. "Many will enter, many will win."
- **Decoy Offer**: Advertise something free/discounted, then present a far more valuable premium offer alongside it. "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?"
- **Buy X Get Y Free**: Reframe pricing so free stuff outnumbers paid stuff. "Buy 1 pair, get 2 free" > "3 pairs for $600."
- **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later**: Pay full price later (with conditional guarantee) OR pay 20-50% less now with bonuses. Gets card on file either way.

## Key Concepts
- **Discount continuum**: Free / $1 / discount exist on a spectrum — interchangeable in any attraction offer
- **Free Goodwill Offer**: Give something valuable for free to build trust and reputation — your free stuff defines your brand more than your paid stuff
- **Store credit > cash refund**: Testing showed store credit and cash back attract the same number of customers

## Mental Models
- Use **Win Your Money Back** when customers need sustained effort to get results (fitness, skills, business building)
- Use **Giveaways** when you want massive lead volume with built-in qualification
- Use **Decoy Offers** when you have a premium version that's clearly superior
- Use **Buy X Get Y Free** when customers can buy more of the same thing (time, quantity, duration)
- Use **Pay Less Now** when you can deliver a clear yes/no result within a short timeframe

## Anti-patterns
- **Grand Prize not grand enough**: If your giveaway doesn't work, the prize wasn't compelling enough for the audience
- **Giving all credit up front**: Apply store credit over time (e.g., $600 over 12 months = $50/mo discount) rather than 3 free months — keeps skin in the game
- **Not having downsells ready**: Some won't buy even with the promotional offer — always have a secondary option

## Key Takeaways
1. The point of Attraction Offers is to turn strangers into customers AND get enough cash up front to cover acquisition costs multiple times over.
2. Win Your Money Back works best for programs requiring sustained effort — about 10% will ask for refunds, but the extra customers more than offset it.
3. Giveaways: Make the Grand Prize the thing you want everyone to buy, then discount it for everyone else.
4. Decoy Offers: Make the contrast HUGE between decoy and premium. Strip the decoy down; load the premium up.
5. Buy X Get Y Free: Always give MORE free things than paid things. "Buy 1 Get 2" beats "Buy 2 Get 1."
6. Pay Less Now: If >10% of "pay later" people cancel, you promised too much, guarantee conditions are too low, or price is too high.
