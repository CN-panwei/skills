# Chapter 1: Money Models Foundation — What's A Money Model?

## Core Idea
A Money Model is a **sequence of offers**. It finds every opportunity to solve a customer's problem and then offers to solve it — making as many offers as you like, as long as you offer the right thing when customers realize they need it.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Money Model**: A sequence of offers that turns strangers into customers, gets them to spend more, catches those who say no, and keeps them buying. Combines four offer types in order.
- **The Four Offer Types**: Attraction Offers (strangers → customers), Upsell Offers (spend more), Downsell Offers (turn "no" into "yes"), Continuity Offers (keep buying).
- **The 8x/27x Growth Multiplier**: Double customer value, double customer count, double speed of payment = 8x growth. Triple each = 27x.

## Key Concepts
- **Cash starvation cycle**: Spend to get customers → lose money → cut advertising → get fewer customers → float with loans → sell equity → fall behind → lose it all
- **Good Money Model = Option 2**: Get paid fast and grow as much as you please (vs. waiting years to recoup)
- **More money faster = more growth**: If customers pay you faster, you can reinvest faster and grow profitably

## Mental Models
- Use the Rental Car Model when designing your offer sequence — a $19/day rental became $100/day through 5 sequential offers solving real problems
- Think of your Money Model as a "problem-solution chain" — each offer solves one problem and reveals the next

## Anti-patterns
- **Single-offer business**: Only selling one thing is barely a business — it's a front end. Figure out what to offer next.
- **Waiting years to recoup**: If it takes 2 years to make cash back, you'll run out of money unless you already have tons of cash

## Key Takeaways
1. All businesses have Money Models — switch "this won't work for my business" to "how will I make this work?"
2. If a customer asks for their money back — give it back. Don't take blood money.
3. Hard selling is for weak products. Make offers available when the customer has the problem.
4. Any offer can be used on its own, at any time, in any order — but the right sequence makes a $100M Money Model.
5. The fastest path to growth: make customers more valuable, get more of them, and get paid faster — simultaneously.
