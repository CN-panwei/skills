# Chapter 4: Upsell Offers — Do You Want Fries With That?

## Core Idea
Upsells are whatever you offer next. Every offer solves one problem and reveals another — your upsell solves that next problem immediately. Often, upsells make the majority of profit. Without them, your first offer barely breaks even.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Classic Upsell**: "You can't have X without Y!" Solve the problem your previous offer just revealed. Ex: bicycle → helmet → lights → puncture-resistant tires.
- **Menu Upsell**: Combine 4 tactics — Unselling (cross out what they don't need), Prescription Upselling (tell them exactly how to use what they do need), A/B Upsell (ask "chocolate or vanilla?" not "want this?"), Card On File ("wanna use the card on file?").
- **Anchor Upsell**: Present a premium offer at 5-10x the price first → get "The Gasp" → come to the rescue → present your main offer at 1/5 the price. Anchored customers spend more and some buy the premium.
- **Rollover Upsell**: Credit some or all of a customer's previous purchase toward your next, more expensive offer. Use for: re-engaging old customers, rescuing upset customers, stealing competitors' customers, upselling current customers.

## Key Concepts
- **Hyper Buying Cycle**: When customers decide to do something new (weddings, new hobbies, babies), they enter a short window of maximum spending — embrace it, keep making offers.
- **BAMFAM** (Book A Meeting From A Meeting): End every appointment by scheduling the next one — agree on why and when before they leave.
- **"Say No to Say Yes"**: "You don't want anything else, do you?" — people trained to say "no" end up agreeing to the upsell.
- **Price your Rollover Upsell at 4x+ the credit**: This ensures at most a 25% discount, preserving profit.

## Mental Models
- Use **Classic Upsell** when your offer naturally reveals a next problem (car → insurance → gas)
- Use **Menu Upsell** when you have multiple products to offer — especially with consultative selling
- Use **Anchor Upsell** when you have a premium version 5-10x the price — some WILL buy it
- Use **Rollover Upsell** to rescue churned/upset customers and steal competitors' unhappy ones

## Anti-patterns
- **Offering something they don't want**: Upsell fails when the offer is too different or doesn't solve their revealed problem
- **Offering at the wrong time**: Before they've experienced the problem your upsell solves
- **Treating the anchor as fake**: If you don't genuinely present the premium offer like you want them to buy it, the customer won't either — and you lose trust
- **Not upselling prepaid customers**: They are your BEST customers. Keep offering — their wallets have "refreshed"

## Key Takeaways
1. The thing you sell the most isn't always the thing you make the most profit on — McDonald's makes 11.6x more profit through upsells than on the burger alone.
2. Give away bonuses that create upsell opportunities (free earmuffs → $30 storage).
3. The faster people get access to upsell items, the more they'll value them — put it in their hands before they say yes.
4. Name your bundled packages by customer type or result: "Fastest Results Bundle," "Transformation Package."
5. Integrate upsells into delivery — make the next thing you want to sell part of how you deliver the first thing.
6. You can charge for guarantees, warranties, and insurance instead of giving them away free — 30% of customers will pay for what you used to give away.
7. Rollover Upsells saved Hormozi's business — one day of recording personalized winback videos generated ~$1.9M in annual revenue.
