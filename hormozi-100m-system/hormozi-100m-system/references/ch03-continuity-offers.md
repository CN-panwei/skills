# Chapter 3: Continuity Offers — Keep People Buying

## Core Idea
Continuity Offers keep customers paying you month after month. They are the backbone of predictable cash flow and the top layer of a $100M Money Model — without them, you start each month at zero.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **SaaS / Subscription Model**: Charge recurring fees for ongoing access to software, content, or community. The gold standard of continuity.
- **Membership Model**: Recurring access to a group, facility, or service. Works for gyms, clubs, professional associations.
- **Consumable Reorder**: Auto-ship physical products that run out (supplements, food, supplies). The original continuity model.
- **Service Retainer**: Ongoing professional services for a monthly fee (consulting, agency, maintenance).

## Key Concepts
- **Predictable revenue**: Continuity makes revenue predictable, which makes growth plannable
- **Lifetime value amplification**: A $200/month customer staying 18 months = $3,600 vs. a $200 one-time purchase
- **Switching costs**: The longer someone stays, the harder it is to leave — design your continuity to increase switching costs over time
- **Gross margin matters**: Continuity only works if your delivery cost is significantly below your recurring price

## Mental Models
- Use **SaaS/Subscription** when your product gets better the longer someone uses it (data accumulation, network effects)
- Use **Consumable Reorder** when customers physically run out of what you sell
- Use **Service Retainer** when your expertise compounds over time and switching providers is costly

## Anti-patterns
- **High churn continuity**: If customers leave within 3 months, your product isn't delivering enough value — fix the product before scaling the subscription
- **Starting at zero each month**: Without continuity, you must re-earn all revenue every 30 days

## Key Takeaways
1. Continuity Offers guarantee cash keeps coming in month after month — they are the capstone of a complete Money Model.
2. The best continuity offers solve a recurring problem that gets worse if discontinued.
3. Apply Attraction Offer credits over the longest period possible to anchor customers into continuity.
4. Even businesses without obvious subscription potential can create continuity through community, updates, or ongoing support.
5. Combine with Upsell and Downsell Offers to maximize revenue per customer over their lifetime.
