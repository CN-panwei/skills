# Chapter 6: Leads Foundation — Engaged Leads Are The True Output of Advertising

## Core Idea
A lead is simply **a person you can contact**. But leads alone aren't enough — you need **engaged leads**: people who *show* interest in the stuff you sell. Engaged leads are the true output of advertising, and getting more of them is the point of the entire $100M Leads system.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Lead vs. Engaged Lead**: A lead = person you can contact. An engaged lead = person who *shows interest* in what you sell (gives contact info, follows you, replies to your campaign).
- **Core Offer vs. Lead Magnet**: Core Offer = what you sell for money. Lead Magnet = a complete solution to a narrow problem, typically free or low-cost, that reveals another problem solved by your Core Offer.
- **The Problem-Solution Cycle**: Every problem has a solution. Every solution reveals more problems. Smaller problem-solution cycles sit inside larger ones. Pick a narrow problem → solve it → the revealed problem = your Core Offer.

## Key Concepts
- **Lead Magnets Get Leads to Engage**: Like salty pretzels at a bar — solve hunger (narrow problem), create thirst (revealed problem), sell drinks (core offer for money).
- **"A person who pays with their time now is more likely to pay with their money later."**
- **Grand Slam Offers work for free stuff as much or better than paid stuff** — make your lead magnet so insanely good people feel stupid saying no.
- **Advertising = letting people know about your stuff**. It's not just paid ads — it's every way you communicate your offer to potential customers.

## Mental Models
- Think of your lead magnet as a "narrow door into a wide room" — it solves one specific, meaningful problem and naturally leads to your Core Offer.
- Use the **Pretzel Model**: Free/cheap thing → creates new desire → paid thing satisfies that desire.
- **Problem-Solution Cycle**: Start narrow, solve completely, let the next problem surface naturally.

## Anti-patterns
- **Advertising your core offer first when it's expensive**: Try core offer advertising first, but if engagement is low, switch to a lead magnet strategy.
- **Making your lead magnet sucky fluff**: If your free stuff is bad, people assume your paid stuff is bad too — and they'll tell others.
- **Not testing your lead magnet name**: 5x more people read your headline than any other part. Small changes = massive engagement differences ("How to get strangers to want to buy your stuff" beat "Get strangers to buy your stuff" by a wide margin).

## Key Takeaways
1. A lead is a person you can contact. An engaged lead shows interest. Engaged leads are what you want.
2. Lead magnets should be valuable enough that you *could* charge for them — and after consuming, they should want more.
3. The 7 Steps to Creating an Effective Lead Magnet: (1) Pick the narrow problem, (2) Decide how to solve it, (3) Choose delivery format, (4) Test the name, (5) Make it easy to consume, (6) Make it darn good, (7) Give a clear CTA with reasons to act now.
4. Three types of lead magnets: Reveal Problems (diagnosis), Samples & Trials (brief full access), One Step of a Multi-Step Process.
5. Four delivery methods: Software (give them a tool), Information (teach them), Services (do work for free), Physical Products (something they can hold).
6. Multiple formats = 2-4x more leads. $100M Offers has a near-perfect ¼ split between ebooks, physical books, audiobooks, and videos.
7. Give away the secrets, sell the implementation. 99% won't buy, but they'll create (or destroy) your reputation based on your free stuff.
8. Good CTAs have two things: (1) What to do, and (2) Reasons to do it RIGHT NOW (scarcity, urgency, any reason at all).
