# Chapter 8: Lead Getters — Get Others to Generate Leads For You

## Core Idea
Once the Core Four methods work for YOU, the next level is getting OTHERS to do it for you. Lead Getters are people who generate leads on your behalf — customers, employees, agencies, and affiliates. This is the shift from "doing" to "leveraging."

## Frameworks Introduced

### Four Types of Lead Getters

**1. Customers as Lead Getters**
- **Referral Programs**: Reward customers for bringing new customers. The reward should be so good they'd be silly not to refer.
- **Two-sided rewards**: Both the referrer AND the new customer get something valuable.
- **Make it automatic**: Build referrals into your delivery process — "Who else do you know who wants this result?"
- **Best timing**: Ask for referrals at the peak of their satisfaction (right after getting a result).

**2. Employees as Lead Getters**
- **Hire salespeople**: They do warm and cold outreach on your behalf.
- **Train on your script**: Don't let them wing it — use your proven messaging.
- **Compensation alignment**: Pay commission-based to align incentives. The best salespeople want uncapped upside.

**3. Agencies as Lead Getters**
- **Outsource expertise**: You pay for specialized knowledge in paid ads, content creation, or cold outreach.
- **Key principle**: Only hire agencies AFTER you've proven the channel works yourself. Otherwise you can't evaluate their work.
- **Clear metrics**: Define cost-per-lead and cost-per-customer targets before engaging.

**4. Affiliates as Lead Getters**
- **Pay for results, not effort**: Affiliates only get paid when they generate a sale or qualified lead.
- **Give them assets**: Provide proven ad copy, landing pages, and creative — make it as easy as possible for them to promote you.
- **Protect your brand**: Set clear guidelines on how affiliates can (and can't) represent your brand.

## Key Concepts
- **Leverage = doing more with less of your own time**: Lead Getters replace your time with other people's time and money.
- **You must prove the model first**: If you can't generate leads profitably yourself, no one else can either.
- **The assembly line**: Core Four methods (you doing it) → Lead Getters (others doing it) = fully functioning $100M Leads machine.
- **Incentive alignment**: The best lead getter programs make the getter's success directly tied to your success.

## Mental Models
- Think of Lead Getters as **multiplying your Core Four efforts** — each getter adds another instance of your proven lead generation system.
- **Customers → the cheapest lead getters**: They already believe in your product and have warm trust with their networks.
- **Affiliates → the lowest-risk lead getters**: You only pay when they deliver, so there's no upfront cost.
- **Employees → the most controllable lead getters**: You train them, manage them, and own the process.
- **Agencies → the most specialized lead getters**: They bring expertise you don't have, but cost more and need oversight.

## Anti-patterns
- **Hiring agencies before proving the channel yourself**: You won't know if they're doing a good job, and they won't have a proven model to follow.
- **One-sided referral rewards**: If only the referrer gets rewarded, the new customer has no incentive to use their referral link.
- **No affiliate guidelines**: Rogue affiliates can damage your brand with misleading claims and spam tactics.
- **Not tracking getter performance**: If you don't know which getter produces the best leads, you can't optimize.

## Key Takeaways
1. Lead Getters complete the $100M Leads machine — they transform your personal effort into a scalable system.
2. The four types: Customers (cheapest), Employees (most controllable), Agencies (most specialized), Affiliates (lowest risk).
3. Always prove the model yourself first — then hand it to Lead Getters with proven scripts, ads, and processes.
4. Two-sided referral rewards work best — both parties benefit, creating natural momentum.
5. Affiliates need your best assets: give them proven copy, landing pages, and creative to maximize their success (and yours).
6. Track everything: cost per lead by getter type, conversion rate, lifetime value. What gets measured gets improved.
