# Patterns — Reusable Strategy Templates

## Pattern 1: The Full Money Model Builder
**When to use**: You're designing a complete offer sequence for a new or existing business.

**Steps**:
1. Define your **Core Offer** — the main transformation you deliver for money
2. Design your **Attraction Offer** — free/low-cost entry that qualifies leads and generates cash
3. Map the **Problem-Solution Chain** — what problem does your Core Offer solve? What problem does THAT solution reveal? That's your Upsell.
4. Create your **Upsell Offer** — solve the next revealed problem immediately after purchase
5. Design your **Downsell Offer** — payment plan, trial, or feature-reduced version for those who say no
6. Add your **Continuity Offer** — recurring revenue layer that keeps customers paying
7. Score each offer with the **Value Equation** — optimize the weakest component first

**Example (Gym Business)**:
- Attraction: "6-Week Challenge — $0 upfront, earn your money back if you hit your goal"
- Upsell: "Nutrition coaching add-on" (solves the diet problem revealed by the workout program)
- Downsell: "3-payment plan" for those who can't afford the full nutrition package
- Continuity: "Monthly membership" after the challenge ends

## Pattern 2: The Lead Magnet Generator
**When to use**: You need to create a lead magnet to attract engaged leads.

**Steps**:
1. Pick a **narrow problem** your ideal customer has (not the big problem — a sub-problem)
2. Verify: solving this problem **reveals** another problem that your Core Offer solves
3. Choose your **lead magnet type**: Reveal Problem (diagnosis), Sample/Trial, or One Step of Multi-Step Process
4. Choose your **delivery format**: Software (tool), Information (teach), Services (do work), Physical Product (something to hold)
5. Create **3+ variations** across delivery formats and test which gets the most engagement
6. **Name it** with a compelling headline — test multiple names with your audience
7. Make it available in **multiple formats** (text, audio, video) for 2-4x more leads
8. End with a **CTA** pointing to your Core Offer: what to do + reasons to do it NOW

**Example (B2B SaaS)**:
- Narrow problem: "Don't know if their website speed is costing them sales"
- Type: Reveal Problem (diagnosis)
- Delivery: Software (free speed test tool)
- CTA: "Your site is 30% slower than industry average — we can fix it in 48 hours. Book a call this week and get 20% off your first month."

## Pattern 3: The Giveaway Campaign
**When to use**: You want to generate a massive volume of leads quickly.

**Steps**:
1. Choose a **Grand Prize** that is exactly what your ideal customer wants to buy (your premium product/service)
2. Set the **promotional discount** for non-winners (e.g., 50% off, free month, $500 credit)
3. Create the **entry mechanism** — email, phone number, social follow + email
4. Set a **deadline** (urgency) and limit **entries** if possible (scarcity)
5. Run the giveaway for 7-14 days
6. **Everyone who enters but doesn't win** receives the promotional discount via email
7. Follow up with a **downsell** for those who don't use the discount

**Example (Online Course)**:
- Grand Prize: "$5,000 Business Accelerator Program (1 seat)"
- Non-winner offer: "50% off the Accelerator — only available for 72 hours after winner announcement"
- Entry: Email + follow on Instagram
- Duration: 10 days

## Pattern 4: The Win-Your-Money-Back Challenge
**When to use**: You sell a program that requires sustained customer effort (fitness, skills, business building).

**Steps**:
1. Set a **challenge price** that covers your cost of delivery + advertising
2. Define **clear, measurable completion criteria** (e.g., attend 90% of sessions, lose 10 lbs, complete all modules)
3. Set the **refund reward**: cash back, store credit, or free month of continuity
4. Apply **store credit over time** rather than all at once (e.g., $50/month discount for 12 months instead of $600 upfront)
5. Celebrate completions publicly — this becomes your best marketing content
6. **Upsell** completers immediately: "You crushed it — ready for the next level?"

**Example (Fitness)**:
- Price: $600 for 6-week challenge
- Criteria: Attend 4x/week for 6 weeks + lose 5% body weight
- Reward: $600 store credit applied as $50/month for 12 months of membership
- Upsell: Personal training package at completion

## Pattern 5: The Anchor Upsell Sequence
**When to use**: You have a premium offer 5-10x the price of your target offer.

**Steps**:
1. Present the **premium offer first** — with full conviction, as if you genuinely want them to buy it
2. Get **"The Gasp"** — the reaction that tells you they've been anchored
3. **Come to the rescue** — "I know that's not for everyone, which is why we also have..."
4. Present your **target offer** at 1/5 to 1/10 the price — it now feels like a steal
5. Some WILL buy the premium — let them. Don't assume they can't afford it.

**Example (Consulting)**:
- Anchor: "Our full partnership is $50,000 for 12 months of hands-on consulting"
- Target: "But most of our clients start with the $5,000 intensive — 4 weeks of deep strategy work"
- Result: The $5K offer feels like a bargain, and ~5-10% choose the $50K partnership

## Pattern 6: The AI-Powered Offer Optimization Loop
**When to use**: You have an existing offer that isn't converting well enough.

**Steps**:
1. **Score** your offer with the Hormozi-fier Prompt (Value Equation)
2. **Identify** the weakest component (lowest relative score)
3. **Apply the fastest fix**:
   - Low Dream → Reframe the outcome with more emotional specificity
   - Low Success → Add or strengthen your guarantee
   - High Time → Engineer a quick win within the first 24-48 hours
   - High Effort → Replace "do-it-yourself" with "done-for-you" or "done-with-you"
4. **Re-score** — confirm the Offer Score increased
5. **A/B test** the improvement against your current version
6. **Repeat** until Offer Score exceeds 1,000,000

## Pattern 7: The Lead Getter Launch
**When to use**: Your Core Four lead generation is working, and you want to scale through others.

**Steps**:
1. **Document** your proven process — scripts, ads, landing pages, cost-per-lead metrics
2. **Start with customers** — launch a two-sided referral program (both parties rewarded)
3. **Add affiliates** — provide your proven assets, pay only on results
4. **Hire employees** — train on your proven scripts, compensate with uncapped commission
5. **Engage agencies** — only for channels you've proven yourself; set clear cost-per-lead targets
6. **Track** performance by getter type — double down on what works, cut what doesn't

## Pattern 8: The Problem-Solution Chain (Offer Sequencing)
**When to use**: You need to figure out WHAT to offer next in your Money Model.

**Steps**:
1. List the **problem your current offer solves**
2. Ask: "What new problem does solving THIS problem create?" → That's your next offer
3. Ask: "What problem existed BEFORE this problem?" → That's your lead magnet
4. Map the full chain: Lead Magnet → Core Offer → Upsell 1 → Upsell 2 → Continuity
5. At each stage, apply the Value Equation to ensure each offer is irresistible
6. Add Downsell Offers at each stage for those who say no

**Example (Web Design Agency)**:
- Lead Magnet: Free website speed test (reveals slow site problem)
- Core Offer: Website redesign (solves slow/ugly site, reveals content problem)
- Upsell 1: SEO content package (solves content problem, reveals traffic problem)
- Upsell 2: Paid ads management (solves traffic problem, reveals conversion problem)
- Continuity: Monthly website maintenance + optimization retainer
